Louvre. Major events in history
Painting Year Authors Masterpieces Events
1665 Le Brun, Charles Entry of Alexander into Babylon It depicts Alexander entering Babylon in a chariot pulled by two elephants. Terraces of hanging gardens can be seen in the background.
1830 Delacroix, Eugène The Liberty leading the people In the center of the painting is a woman known as Marianne, a symbol of the French Republic and the personification of the national motto "Freedom, Equality, Fraternity". Freedom is followed by representatives of various social classes — the worker, the bourgeois, the teenager — symbolizing the unity of the French people during the July revolution
1799 David, Jacques-Louis The Sabines The painting depicts a battle between the Romans and the Sabines. The central female character of the painting, Hersilia, the wife of Romulus, stood between her father (Tacius, king of the Sabines) and her husband, who had already raised a spear, trying to hit Hersilia's father.
